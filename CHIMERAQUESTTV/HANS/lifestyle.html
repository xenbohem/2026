Tea Time: A Royal Blend of Cultures

Prince Louis combines the elegance of British afternoon tea with the refined manners and presentation of Parisian court customs. His tea rituals are a symbol of his cosmopolitan lifestyle—fusing tradition with sophistication.

Tea Selection & Accompaniments

  • Tea Varieties: Earl Grey, Darjeeling, and a delicate French lavender-infused tisane.
  • Serving Ware: Fine porcelain with floral motifs, silver teapots, and crystal sugar bowls.
  • Pastries & Snacks: French croissants, éclairs, and petits fours alongside British finger sandwiches with herbs, smoked salmon, or foie gras pâté.

Tea Etiquette & Customs

  • Timing: Usually served at 4 pm, mid-afternoon, in the palace gardens or elegant salons.
  • Pouring the Tea: The prince personally artfully pours from the teapot, offering tea to his guests with a gentle, gracious manner.
  • Table Manners: Using fine utensils, delicate napkins, and engaging in polite, subdued conversation.
  • Enjoyment: Savoring each sip and bite, appreciating both the flavors and the artistry of the service.

Additional Touches

The prince values the harmony of cultures in his tea rituals—combining British formality with the Parisian love for beauty, art, and etiquette. His tea time is not just a refreshment but a social event celebrating refinement and international flair.
